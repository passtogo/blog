Documented or Undocumented Citizens: Every Person Has Basic Human Rights

Published on November 12, 2023

Documented or Undocumented, Legal or illegal, Citizen or non-citizen, words used to clasify what rights we have! We're diving into a topic that's often surrounded by serious faces and stern discussions: the rights of documented and undocumented citizens. But fear not, because we're about to sprinkle a little lightness onto this heavy conversation.

First things first, let's get this out of the way: we all have rights. Yes, you, me, your neighbor, and even that person you see at the grocery store every Sunday. It doesn't matter if you're carrying a passport the size of a novel or if you've got a collection of stamps from around the world — human rights are like the VIP pass to the global party, and everyone's invited.

Now, the whole "documented" and "undocumented" talk can get a bit confusing, like trying to assemble furniture with instructions that only make sense in another dimension. But fear not! The key is recognizing that, at the end of the day, we're all human beings with a bunch of rights in our back pockets.

Picture this: you've got a universal remote control, and guess what? It's loaded with human rights channels. These channels are the same for everyone, whether you're binge-watching documentaries on Netflix or sitting in a cozy café in Paris.

One essential channel on this universal remote is the "Dignity and Respect" channel. It doesn't matter if you're a seasoned explorer with stamps from every continent or if your passport has seen better days — you deserve to be treated with dignity and respect. It's like the unwritten rule of the global etiquette handbook.

Next up, we've got the "Fair Play" channel. This one is all about justice and due process. Imagine it as the referee in the game of life, ensuring that everyone gets a fair shot. So, whether you're wearing a suit, a sombrero, or a kimono, you're entitled to a fair and impartial hearing. Game on!

Education is like the "Learning & Growing" channel. Every kiddo, no matter where they're from or where they're headed, has the right to access education. It's like the superpower that transforms societies and makes the world a cooler place. So, let's make sure everyone gets a seat in this classroom of life.

Now, let's talk about the elephant in the room – immigration policies. We get it; it's a touchy subject. But imagine if we could approach it like planning a massive potluck. Everyone brings something unique to the table, and with a bit of communication and understanding, we can create a feast that satisfies everyone's cravings.

Communities, that's where you come in! Be the hosts of the global block party. Start conversations, share stories, and dispel myths. It's like spicing up the potluck with flavors from every corner of the world. Let's build communities that throw the best parties, where everyone feels welcome.

In the grand symphony of life, let's remember that our shared humanity is the melody that ties us all together. Documented or undocumented, with passports stamped or pristine, we're all part of the same global playlist.

So, the next time you find yourself in a serious conversation about the rights of documented and undocumented citizens, throw in a sprinkle of lightness. Because at the end of the day, we're all navigating this wild ride called life, and a little laughter can make the journey a whole lot brighter. Cheers to human rights, diversity, and the incredible adventure of being alive!

Final words

For a country born under the idea of unity, justice, tranquility, defense, welfare and liberty to secure its prosperity, sorting people to give some those opening lines and ignore the rest is history repeating itself. It seems we lack the knowledge or skill to learn from past mistakes. What is it that makes a country born out of those moral ideals not strive to reach its goal laid out on its birth certificate? Why build a wall in our own community? The fact is we won’t reach anywhere where the founding fathers of this country set out to steer it.
